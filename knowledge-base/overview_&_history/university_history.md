# University of Louisiana at Lafayette — History

---

## Timeline of Names

| Period | Name |
|---|---|
| 1898–1921 | Southwestern Louisiana Industrial Institute (SLII) |
| 1921–1960 | Southwestern Louisiana Institute (SLI) |
| 1960–1999 | University of Southwestern Louisiana (USL) |
| 1999–Present | University of Louisiana at Lafayette (UL Lafayette) |

---

## Southwestern Louisiana Industrial Institute (SLII) — 1898–1921

UL Lafayette was originally named the **Southwestern Louisiana Industrial Institute (SLII)**, created by a legislative act on July 14, 1898. State Senator Robert Martin — for whom the administrative building Martin Hall is named — authored this legislation.

- Several towns competed to host the school; **Lafayette** was chosen due to a land donation of 25 acres from the Girard family, a $8,000 cash donation, and a ten-year property tax offered by the town.
- **1899:** Board of Trustees established.
- **1900:** Construction began; **Dr. Edwin Stephens** (age 27) named president.
- **1901:** Classes began with 100 students. Stephens planted the famous Twentieth Century Oaks (still lining Johnston Street and University Avenue). He later founded the national Live Oak Society.
- **1904:** UL Lafayette Alumni Association formed; first issue of student newspaper *The Vermilion* published.
- **1909:** Laboratory school added for teacher training.
- Early graduates included **Jefferson Caffery**, who became known as the "Dean of U.S. Diplomats" serving as ambassador to France, Brazil, and Egypt. Another early alumnus **Keener Cagle** won the award later known as the Heisman Trophy.

---

## Southwestern Louisiana Institute (SLI) — 1921–1960

- **1921:** Constitutional Convention dropped "Industrial" from the name; institution authorized to grant **bachelor's degrees**. Campus doubled to 50 acres.
- **1922:** Extension courses offered in surrounding parishes.
- **1925:** Accredited by the **Southern Association of Colleges and Schools (SACS)**; student government association formed.
- **1938:** Dr. Stephens retired after 38 years as president, with enrollment exceeding 2,000 students.
- **1941–1944:** WWII crisis — Navy's V-12 and V-5 officer training programs saved faculty jobs and academic programs. SLI won the first Oil Bowl (1943).
- **Early 1940s:** College of Engineering organized.
- **Post-WWII:** 108 units of veterans housing purchased; "Vet Village" created for married student housing.
- **1951:** College of Nursing founded.
- **1952:** College of Business Administration founded.
- **1954:** SLI became the **first college in Louisiana to integrate its student body**. La Maison Acadienne Française acquired (center for south Louisiana French culture).
- **1956:** Graduate programs approved — the beginning of the university era.

---

## University of Southwestern Louisiana (USL) — 1960–1999

- **1960:** State legislature approved renaming to **University of Southwestern Louisiana**. Composed of graduate school and six colleges: agriculture, business administration, education, engineering, liberal arts, and nursing. Enrollment approaching 5,000.
- **1961:** First digital computer acquired.
- **1964:** Master's program in computer science launched.
- **1966:** Dr. Clyde Rougeou becomes president (served until 1974). Campus expanded dramatically.
- **1969:** First doctoral degree in **computer science** — one of the earliest in the nation. Other doctorates: mathematics, biology, history, microbiology, statistics, English.
- **1973:** Center for Louisiana Studies founded to research and preserve south Louisiana's French-based culture (published 125+ books on Louisiana).
- **1974:** Dr. Ray Authement becomes president; all athletics elevated to **Division I**. Authement would serve for 34 years — the longest-serving president of a public university in the country.
- **1984:** Center for Advanced Computer Studies formed. New Iberia Research Center (one of the nation's largest primate centers) acquired.
- **1990s:** National Wetlands Research Center located in the UL Lafayette Research Park; National Marine Fisheries Service Facility established.
- **1994:** One of only three **doctoral programs in Francophone studies** in the world launched.

---

## University of Louisiana at Lafayette (UL Lafayette) — 1999–Present

- **September 10, 1999:** President Authement officially signed the order changing the name to **University of Louisiana at Lafayette** during the Centennial Celebration.
- **1999:** TOPS (Taylor Opportunity Program for Students) implemented; 2,000+ students received full tuition. University adopted **selective admissions standards**.
- **1999:** Institute for Cognitive Science established (with Dr. Daniel Povinelli, a James S. McDonnell Centennial Fellow).
- **2000–2015:** Major campus construction boom — 18 new buildings, 17 renovations/expansions:
  - Moody Hall (B.I. Moody III College of Business Administration)
  - Legacy Park (on-campus student apartments)
  - Hilliard Art Museum ($8.4 million)
  - Cecil J. Picard Center for Child Development and Lifelong Learning
  - Louisiana Immersive Technologies Enterprise (LITE) — world's first fully digital 3-D data visualization facility
  - Oliver Hall (Computer Science)
  - New Student Union ($36 million)
  - Bourgeois Hall Student Aquatic Center
  - Child Development Center
  - Multiple parking towers and new dormitories

- **2001:** Joined Southeastern Universities Research Association (SURA). New degree programs: Doctorate in Communicative Disorders and Master of Architecture.
- **2005:** Named to Princeton Review's Best Colleges Guide for the first time. Hurricanes Katrina and Rita — UL Lafayette played major relief role.
- **2006:** LITE Center opened — world's first completely digital 3-D data visualization facility.
- **2008:** Dr. Ray Authement retired after 34 years. **Dr. E. Joseph Savoie** became president.
- **2009:** Named **Tree Campus USA**; major renovation of Burke-Hawthorne Hall (KRVS radio station & Communication Department).
- **2010:** Ernest J. Gaines Center opened (Gaines was writer-in-residence 1981–2004). Named one of the country's top 100 research universities.
- **2011–2012:** New dormitories constructed; campus housing capacity increased by 37% (housing for ~3,000 students).
- **2012:** Center for Visual and Decision Informatics (CVDI) established — Louisiana's first NSF Industry/University Cooperative Research Center. Named most affordable four-year public university in Louisiana by U.S. Department of Education.
- **2013:** Ranked No. 4 in the country for social mobility (Washington Monthly).
- **Current:** Carnegie R1 classification; $4.4 billion economic impact; 16,100 enrolled students; **Dr. Ramesh Kolluru** serving as President.

## Notable Historical Alumni

- **Jefferson Caffery** — "Dean of U.S. Diplomats," served as ambassador to France, Brazil, and Egypt
- **Keener Cagle** — won the award later known as the Heisman Trophy
- **Christiana Smith** — first African American graduate (honored with an alumni chapter in her name)
- **Ernest J. Gaines** — famous African American novelist, writer-in-residence 1981–2004 ("A Gathering of Old Men," "A Lesson Before Dying")
