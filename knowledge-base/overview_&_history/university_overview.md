## About the University

The **University of Louisiana at Lafayette (UL Lafayette)** is a national research institution located in the heart of culturally and ecologically diverse South Louisiana. It is the largest institution in the University of Louisiana System and the second-largest university in the state of Louisiana (behind Louisiana State University).

UL Lafayette holds the prestigious **Carnegie R1 classification** — the same designation shared by Harvard, Duke, Johns Hopkins, and Princeton — placing it among the top 3% of public and private universities in the United States for research activity.

The university is deeply rooted in the Cajun and Creole culture of Acadiana, and that heritage infuses its academic programs, student traditions, and community engagement efforts. Students enjoy a personal, engaging educational experience while also benefiting from the resources and opportunities of a large research university.

## Mission & Values

UL Lafayette's mission is to educate students, advance knowledge through research, and serve the regional, national, and global community. The university:

- Advances research and innovation for the betterment of society and the world
- Fosters a welcoming, diverse community
- Cultivates creativity and collaboration among students, faculty, and staff
- Holds true to its Cajun and Creole cultural values and traditions
