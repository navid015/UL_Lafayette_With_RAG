## Housing

**Website:** https://housing.louisiana.edu  
**Apply:** https://housing.louisiana.edu/apply  
**Contact:** oncampusliving@louisiana.edu (to schedule tours)  
**Director of Residential Life:** Maylen Aldana

### At a Glance
- **3,723** beds on campus
- **9** residence halls (all built or renovated within the last 10 years)
- Room styles available: Traditional, Suite, Junior Suite, Apartment, Family housing
- Utilities included: cable, internet, electricity, garbage, water
- Free laundry facilities with text notification system
- Staff provides housekeeping for suite-style bathrooms/common areas
- Cajun Village: family apartment housing with adjacent Child Development Center

### Housing Types

| Type | Description |
|---|---|
| Traditional Halls | Classic double-occupancy rooms with shared hall bathrooms |
| Junior Suite-Style | Semi-private suites; shared bathroom between 2 rooms |
| Suite-Style | Private suites; bedroom + private or semi-private bathroom |
| Apartment-Style (Legacy Park) | On-campus apartments for upperclassmen |
| Family Housing (Cajun Village) | Apartments for students with families |

### Living-Learning Communities (LLCs)
Freshmen can live and take classes with students sharing similar interests/majors. Programs available for various majors and interest groups. Learn more: https://firstyear.louisiana.edu/get-involved/living-communities

### Housing Scholarships
- Live Oak Scholarship: $5,960/semester for 2 semesters (suite-style unit) for qualifying freshmen
- Magnolia Scholarship: $2,734/semester for 2 semesters (junior suite) for qualifying freshmen
- Marching Band: housing scholarship available for Pride of Acadiana members

## Living in Lafayette, Louisiana

Lafayette is a vibrant, culturally rich city — the hub of Cajun/Creole culture:

| Recognition | Source |
|---|---|
| #1 Best College Town Weekend | USA Today Readers' Choice |
| One of America's Most Artistic Towns | Expedia |
| Hip, Historic College Town in the South | ConventionSouth Magazine |

**Cultural Highlights:**
- Home of Festival International de Louisiane (the largest international music & arts festival in the U.S.)
- World-renowned Cajun and Creole cuisine
- Live music everywhere — zydeco, Cajun, jazz, blues
- Art galleries, museums (Hilliard Art Museum on campus)
- Girard Park — major green space adjacent to campus
- Downtown Lafayette within walking distance

**Lafayette's Economy:**
- Growing hub for oil & gas, healthcare, and technology companies
- Major employers include Acadian Ambulance, CGI, Schumacher Group, GE Capital, and international energy companies

## Costs & Financial Considerations

| Cost Item | Notes |
|---|---|
| Tuition (in-state) | Below average for Louisiana public universities; most affordable 4-year public university in Louisiana (2012 U.S. Dept. of Education) |
| Tuition (out-of-state) | Out-of-state fee waivers available through scholarship programs |
| Online Tuition | Flat-rate per-credit-hour; no additional out-of-state fees for online students (e.g., MSN online: $480/credit hour) |
| Room & Board | Included in housing plan with utilities |
| Meal Plans | Available; various options |

**Affordability Recognitions:**
- Most affordable 4-year public university in Louisiana (U.S. Dept. of Education, 2012)
- #4 in the nation for social mobility (Washington Monthly, 2013)
- Top 16% for helping low-income students (Washington Monthly)
- Forbes Best Value College
