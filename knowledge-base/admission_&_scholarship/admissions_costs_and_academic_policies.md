### General Requirements
- Bachelor's degree from an accredited institution
- Minimum undergraduate GPA (varies by program, typically 2.5-3.0)
- GRE (required for some programs, not required for others — check specific program)
- Letters of recommendation
- Personal statement/essay
- Transcripts from all institutions attended

### International Applicants (Additional Requirements)
- TOEFL or IELTS scores
- Credential evaluation for non-U.S. degrees
- Financial documentation
- For scholarships: Apply by June 20 (Fall) or December 16 (Spring)

## Admissions Standards

UL Lafayette adopted **selective admissions standards** in 1999 (first-time freshmen). In 2012, standards were raised further — resulting in an increase (not decrease) in enrollment, along with improved retention and graduation rates.

**General Freshman Requirements:**
- High school diploma or equivalent
- ACT composite of 17+ (or equivalent SAT), OR
- High school GPA meeting specified criteria
- Specific scholarship eligibility requires higher scores (see Scholarship file)

## Costs & Tuition

### Tuition & Fees Overview
UL Lafayette is known for affordability:
- **Most Affordable 4-Year Public University in Louisiana** (U.S. Dept. of Education College Affordability and Transparency List, 2012)
- **Forbes Best Value College** (net price, school quality, alumni earnings)
- **Top 16%** for helping low-income students (Washington Monthly)
- **#4 in the Nation for Social Mobility** (Washington Monthly, 2013)

**Important:** Costs change annually. Consult the official Bursar website or contact the Financial Aid Office for current rates.

## TOPS — Taylor Opportunity Program for Students

Louisiana's state scholarship for high-performing in-state students. Three levels:
- **TOPS Opportunity** — Minimum requirements
- **TOPS Performance** — Higher requirements; tuition + $400/year stipend
- **TOPS Honors** — Highest requirements; tuition + $800/year stipend

**How to Apply:** File the FAFSA  
**Provider:** Louisiana Office of Student Financial Assistance

## Important Academic Policies

### GPA Requirements
- Students must maintain a minimum GPA to keep academic scholarships (varies by scholarship)
- Academic scholarships require continued enrollment in specified minimum credit hours per semester

### Scholarship Retention
Each scholarship may have specific GPA and enrollment requirements. Key requirements for most scholarships:
- 3.0 cumulative GPA (for freshman scholarships)
- Full-time enrollment each semester (varies by scholarship)
- Must maintain good academic standing

### Selective Admissions
- All applicants must meet UL Lafayette's admissions standards
- Students not meeting requirements may be referred to alternative pathways

### Online Programs
- Online students are not charged out-of-state fees
- Does not apply to Compete LA programs

### Transfer Credit
- Credit evaluation done at time of admission
- College-level credits with grade of C or better typically transfer
