## Scholarships Overview

- **$20 million** in scholarship dollars awarded annually
- **73%** of first-time undergraduate students received state grants or scholarships
- **63%** of freshmen received an academic scholarship
- No separate scholarship application required — students are **automatically considered** when they apply for admission
- Scholarship eligibility is reviewed based on test scores and GPA once admitted

---

## 1. First-Time Freshman Scholarships (2026-2027)

All freshman scholarships require:
- 3.0 cumulative, non-weighted high school GPA
- 23 ACT composite/superscore OR 1130 SAT composite
- U.S. citizenship
- Regular admission to the University
- All requirements must be met by **May 31st** of the graduating year
- Scholarships are offered for **8 semesters** (unless noted otherwise)

| Criteria | Detail |
|---|---|
| ACT Score | 32 or higher |
| SAT Score | 1420 or higher |
| GPA | 3.0+ cumulative |
| Award | $3,000/semester (8 semesters) |
| Freshman Housing | $5,960/semester (2 semesters, suite-style shared unit) |
| Campus Job (optional) | Up to $1,200/semester |
| **Total Estimated Value** | **$45,520** |

## 3. Transfer Student Scholarships (2026-2027)

General eligibility for all transfer scholarships:
- U.S. citizenship
- 12+ hours of college-level coursework
- Must be eligible to return to previous institution
- Must not be a transfer re-entry student
- Must not possess a bachelor's degree

### Academic Transfer Scholarships

| Scholarship | Eligibility | Award |
|---|---|---|
| **Academic Transfer Scholarship** | 12+ credit hours; 3.2+ cumulative GPA | $700/semester × 6 semesters |
| **Associate Degree Transfer Scholarship** | Associate Degree; 3.0+ cumulative GPA | $950–$1,200/semester × 6 semesters |

### Supplemental Transfer Scholarships

| Scholarship | Eligibility | Award |
|---|---|---|
| **Out-of-State Transfer Scholarship** | 12+ credit hours; 2.75+ cumulative GPA | Full non-resident fee exemption ($6,864/semester × 10 semesters) |
| **Phi Theta Kappa Scholarship** | Associate Degree; 3.0+ GPA; Active PTK membership | $500/semester × 4 semesters |

## 5. Louisiana TOPS Program (Taylor Opportunity Program for Students)

TOPS is Louisiana's scholarship for high-performing in-state students. There are **three levels**:

| Level | Description |
|---|---|
| TOPS Opportunity | Pays full tuition for students meeting basic requirements |
| TOPS Performance | Higher ACT/GPA requirements; pays tuition plus $400/year stipend |
| TOPS Honors | Highest requirements; pays tuition plus $800/year stipend |

- **How to apply:** File the FAFSA (Free Application for Federal Student Aid)
- **Provider:** Louisiana Office of Student Financial Assistance

## 10. Federal & State Grants

### Pell Grant
- Need-based; amount varies by financial need
- For first-time degree-seeking students
- Apply via FAFSA

### Federal Supplemental Educational Opportunity Grant (FSEOG)
- Typically ~$2,600/year
- Must be enrolled in 6+ credit hours and eligible for maximum Pell Grant
- Apply via FAFSA

### Iraq & Afghanistan Service Grant (IASG)
- For students whose parent died in military service in Iraq/Afghanistan after 9/11
- Apply via FAFSA

### Louisiana Go Grant
- For Louisiana residents who are also Pell Grant eligible
- Apply via FAFSA
