## Traditions & Campus Culture

| Tradition | Description |
|---|---|
| **Lagniappe Day** | Annual spring celebration with canoe races in Cypress Lake, 20,000-lb crawfish boil, and live music |
| **Festival International de Louisiane** | Largest international music & arts festival in U.S.; 5 days, 7 stages, overlaps with finals week |
| **Krewe of Roux** | University kick-off to Mardi Gras season; masquerade ball & campus parade |
| **Downtown Alive!** | Downtown Lafayette free Friday night concert series |
| **Cypress Lake** | Beloved campus landmark with live cypress trees; site of Lagniappe Day canoe races |
| **Walk of Honor** | Brick-paved campus path with 80,000+ commemorative bricks for UL graduates (1903–2000) |
| **Twentieth Century Oaks** | Live oaks planted by founding President Dr. Edwin Stephens on January 1, 1901 |
| **The Vermilion** | Student newspaper, founded 1904 |
| **KRVS** | Campus radio station (NPR affiliate), housed in Burke-Hawthorne Hall |
| **Pride of Acadiana** | The Marching Band |
